5 Signs You Need to Rekey Your Locks

By CCR Locksmith Team • Published December 20, 2025 • Updated March 4, 2026

Rekeying changes the internal pins of a lock so old keys stop working and new keys are issued. It is often faster and more affordable than replacing all hardware.

Top Signs

  1. You moved into a new home or office.
  2. Keys were lost, stolen, or unreturned.
  3. There was employee or tenant turnover.
  4. Your key control has become unclear (too many copies).
  5. You want one key to work across selected doors.

When to Replace Instead

If the lock body is damaged, badly worn, or no longer meets your security requirements, replacement may be the better option.
